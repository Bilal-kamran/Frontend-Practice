Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

  • Yields: About 2 dozen cookies
  • Prep Time: 15 minutes
  • Cook Time: 10-12 minutes per batch

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup chocolate chips (mild chocolate, semi-sweet, or a mix)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Cream butter and sugars: In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and both sugars together with an electric mixer until light creamy (this usually takes 2-3 minutes).
    Tip: Make sure your butter is truly softened, not melted, for the best texture.
  3. Beat in egg and vanilla: Beat in egg until well combined, then stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Whisk dry ingredients: In a seperate medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
    Tip: Whisking helps to evenly distribute the baking soda and salt.
  5. Combine wet and dry ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing on low speed until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; overmixing can lead to tough cookies.
  6. Stir in chocolate chips: Fold in the chocolate chips until evenly distributed throughout the dough.
    Tip: For extra flavor, try using a mix of chocolate chips or even chunks.
  7. Drop onto baking sheets: Drop rounded tablespoon of cookie dough onto the prepared baking sheets, leaving about 2 inches between each cookie.
    Tip: Use a cookie scoop for uniform-sized cookies.
  8. Bake: Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the centers still look slightly soft.
    Tip: Don't overbake! The cookies will continue to set as they cool.
  9. Cool: Let the cookies cool on the baking sheets for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Enjoy!
    Tip: For chewier cookies, slightly underbake them. For cripier cookies, bake them for an extra minute or two.
